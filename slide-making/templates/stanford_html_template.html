Inventory drift dominates spread economics

The decomposition

net P&L (this 10-minute window)
spread captured: 50 fills × $0.05 × 100 sh  =  +$250
net inventory: long 1,000 sh × $1.00 drop  =  −$1,000
net adverse selection on informed slice  =  −$200
net = −$950
The hedge: sell 1,000 sh at $379.95 immediately. You take the loss but stop the bleed. The kill switch is the second-order defense.
Spread is gross revenue. Net = spread − adverse selection − inventory drift − fees.

Why widening the spread isn't always the answer

Adverse selection vs. fill rate

Critical formula. Required spread $s$ satisfies $(1{-}p)\cdot s \geq p \cdot \mathrm{informed\ loss}$. With $p=30\%$ and informed move $=\$0.10$: $s \geq \$0.043$/side, so quoted spread $\geq \$0.086$.
1.3 Single-stock alpha trader City 3 of 5 · D1

"AAPL is worth $215. It trades at $200."

Hardest business in District 1. Most over-claimed.

Kelly sizing under uncertainty
$f^* = (p \cdot b - q) / b$
Sharpe 0.8, 90-day → ~25% of capital. Half-Kelly safer for parameter uncertainty.
capacity decay
a 5%/yr signal at $10M is ~1%/yr at $1B
Capacity test: how much does each next $10M shrink expected alpha?
FINRA 5270 boundary — research desk hoot is not actionable info if you have an info barrier and act on the public news that prompted it. MS 2024 settled $249M on exactly this line.
